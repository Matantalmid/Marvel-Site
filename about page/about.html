The man who started it all: STAN LEE

Stan Lee was an American comic book legend who changed the face of the genre. He created or co-created some of the world's most beloved characters, including Spider-Man, the Hulk, Daredevil, the Fantastic Four, and the X-Men. His work was groundbreaking in its portrayal of complex and socially relevant characters, and it has influenced generations of comic book artists and writers.

1993-2007: The Beginning

Marvel Studios was founded in 1993 by Avi Arad, Michael Eisner, and Ike Perlmutter. The studio started as a production company for comic book-based films, but they faced challenges early on. Their first films, such as Daredevil (2003) and Elektra (2005), were not commercially or critically successful.

2008-2012: The Success of the MCU

The year that everything changed for Marvel Studios was 2008. The studio released the film Iron Man, which was a huge commercial and critical success. The film introduced the world to the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), a series of films connected by shared characters, storylines, and events.

2013-2019: Continued Success

The MCU continued to be successful in the years following, with films such as Captain America: The Winter Soldier (2014), Avengers: Age of Ultron (2015), Captain America: Civil War (2016), Avengers: Infinity War (2018), and Avengers: Endgame (2019). These films were huge commercial and critical successes, making the MCU one of the most successful media franchises in the world.

2020-Present: The Future

The MCU continues to expand with new films such as Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 (2023), Black Widow (2023), Thor: Love and Thunder (2022), and the X-Men (2023). The studio is also working on new television series for Disney+, such as WandaVision (2021), Loki (2021), and Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings (2021).
